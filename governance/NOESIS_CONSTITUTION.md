# Noesis Constitution

## Article 1 — Separation of governance layers

Noesis constitutional governance controls identity, lifecycle, provenance, auditability, trust zones, release, allowed use, and Logos export.

Branch-native governance controls how a branch understands its own authority structure.

## Article 2 — No AI self-canonization

AI may generate candidates. AI may not promote itself, approve its own outputs, rewrite root authority, or become canonical source of truth.

## Article 3 — No universal branch collapse

Branches must not be flattened when internal schools, sects, rites, or lineages materially change authority, practice, or governance.

## Article 4 — Auditability

Every canonical change must be attributable, reversible or tombstoned, and linked to provenance.

## Article 5 — Logos boundary

Logos compatibility is opt-in and reviewer-gated. No branch may force a Logos interpretation.
